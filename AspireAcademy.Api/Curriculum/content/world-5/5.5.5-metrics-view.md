# Metrics View

The Metrics view renders time-series charts for every metric instrument reported by your application. Both built-in metrics from Aspire integrations and custom metrics from your own `Meter` instances appear here.

## Navigation Hierarchy

Metrics are organized in a three-level hierarchy:

1. **Resource** — Select which service to inspect.
2. **Meter** — Choose a meter within that service (e.g., `Microsoft.AspNetCore.Hosting`, `System.Net.Http`, or your custom `MyApp.Orders`).
3. **Instrument** — Select a specific instrument to view its chart (e.g., `http.server.request.duration`, `orders.placed`).

This hierarchy keeps the view organized even when dozens of meters and instruments are reporting data simultaneously.

## Chart Types by Instrument

Different instrument types render as different visualizations:

- **Counters** — Show two views: cumulative total over time and a per-second rate. The rate view is most useful for understanding current throughput.
- **Histograms** — Display percentile breakdowns: p50 (median), p90, and p99. This reveals the distribution of values, highlighting tail latency or outlier behavior.
- **Gauges** — Show the current point-in-time value. Useful for things like active connections, queue depth, or memory usage.

## Tags and Dimensions

If your metrics include tags (e.g., `region`, `status_code`), the dashboard lets you filter or group by those dimensions. A counter tagged with `region=us-east` and `region=eu-west` can be viewed as separate series on the same chart.

## Custom Meters

Any custom `Meter` registered with `.WithMetrics(m => m.AddMeter("MyApp.Orders"))` appears alongside the built-in .NET meters. Your business metrics—orders placed, revenue processed, cache hit ratios—are visualized with the same charting as framework-level metrics, giving you a unified view of both infrastructure and business health.
